title: "Exploring Synthetic Visual Data for Training Deep-Learning Based Classifiers"
conference: Preprint
abstract: A number of recent papers examine the utility of synthetic visual data in niche deep learning tasks with the hope of understanding whether synthetic data can become a fundamental instrument for deep learning tasks. The current proof-of-concept research examines the utility of synthetic visual data across multiple datasets and multiple deep learning-based computer vision architectures. The experiment uses StyleGAN2-ADA, a generative adversarial network tailored for small datasets, to generate synthetic datasets from examples drawn from the CIFAR10, MNIST, and ImageNet 32 datasets. StyleGAN2-ADA was trained on each dataset until the FID score was constant for more than 50 epochs. The generated synthetic datasets were used to train three classification models. Each model was trained with solely authentic imagery, a mix of synthetic and authentic imagery, and solely synthetic imagery to evaluate the efficacy of synthetic data. The experiment shows that while authentic training data results in the highest accuracy, classification models trained partially or solely on synthetic data appear to perform consistently. Each dataset results in similar outcomes across classifier architectures, and a mix of synthetic and authentic data demonstrate consistent performance outcomes across models. Datasets with large data dimensionality and/or complexity comparatively do worse. Future experiments to explore improvements in synthetic data generation are needed to close the domain gap between the accuracy of models trained on authentic and synthetic data.
